Upper Body

Push Ups for upper body

    • Push-ups generally target the chest, shoulders, and triceps, but they also engage the core and stabilizer muscles.

    • Push-ups are a versatile exercise that can be done anywhere, making them ideal for at-home training.

    • Many push-up variations (such as incline, decline, diamond, and wide-grip) can target different muscle regions while increasing or decreasing difficulty.

    Blog 2:-

    Lower Body

    Squats for lower body

      • Squats work many muscular groups, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves, making them an excellent exercise for developing general lower-body strength.

      • Squats improve functional movement patterns by simulating daily tasks such as sitting and standing. This enhances balance, coordination, and mobility in daily activities.

      • Squats can be adjusted to increase or decrease difficulty, such as adding weights (barbell or dumbbell) for additional resistance or doing variations like sumo squats or single-leg squats to target different muscles and adapt to varied fitness levels.
